## Email 2 — Day 2
**Subject:** The accountability gap that quietly kills AI programmes
**Preview text:** Everyone has a stake. Almost no one has accountability.

Hi {name},

A pattern I see again and again across the UAE and the wider region:

An AI initiative involves IT, data science, the business unit, legal, compliance, procurement — sometimes the board. Everyone has a stake. **Very few have accountability.**

When something goes wrong (and in any serious initiative, something always does), decisions slow to a crawl. Escalations bounce between functions. The timeline slips. Momentum dies.

The fix is simple to say and hard to do: **name one person who owns the outcome, has the authority to decide, and is measured on delivery.** Not a committee. Not a shared mailbox. One accountable owner.

Think of it like a turnaround on an oil & gas platform — nobody runs a planned shutdown without a single Turnaround Manager who owns the schedule, the budget and every decision. AI programmes deserve the same discipline.

More soon,
Vijay

## Email 3 — Day 5
**Subject:** Why governance-first organisations move *faster*, not slower
**Preview text:** Counterintuitive, but it's the pattern that holds.

Hi {name},

There's a belief I'd like to challenge: that AI governance slows you down.

In my experience it's the opposite. The organisations that build governance *first* — data provenance, explainability, human oversight, clear accountability — deploy AI **faster**, because they've removed the late-stage legal and risk blockers that stall everyone else.

This matters more than ever in the UAE. With TDRA, ADGM and DIFC expectations tightening — and a dedicated UAE AI Law on the way — governance is shifting from "nice to have" to a condition of operating.

The mistake is treating it as a compliance checkbox bolted on at the end. The organisations getting it right treat governance as **competitive advantage**: the thing that lets them move with confidence while competitors are stuck in review cycles.

## Notes for setup

- **Cadence:** Days 0, 2, 5, 9, 14. Don't compress tighter — executives need breathing room.
- **Send time:** ~8:00–9:00 GST (start of the UAE working day, Sun–Thu).
- **Stop rule:** If someone books a call (or replies), remove them from the automation so they don't keep getting nurture emails.
- **One list, two entry points:** Both the guide download and the assessment can feed the same automation. Optionally branch the wording for assessment-completers (they've already seen the readiness angle, so lead Email 4 differently).
- **Arabic:** If you want an Arabic-language version of the sequence for Arabic-first leads, say the word and I'll translate all five.
- **Deliverability:** Authenticate your domain (SPF/DKIM) in MailerLite/Brevo so these land in the inbox, not spam. Both tools walk you through it.
